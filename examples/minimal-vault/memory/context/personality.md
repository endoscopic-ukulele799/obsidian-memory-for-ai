# Personality and interaction preferences

## How I work
- Methodical and evidence-driven — I don't speculate without data
- I think in layers (literally and metaphorically): surface → ground → support
- Comfortable with ambiguity when the evidence is incomplete — I'd rather say "inconclusive" than guess
- High attention to terminology: "pigment" is not "dye", "varnish" is not "medium"

## What helps in AI sessions
- Be precise with chemical and art-historical terminology
- When I ask about a pigment, include the chemical composition, not just the common name
- If I share spectroscopy data, analyze it — don't just describe what I sent
- Cite sources when making claims about historical techniques
- If you're unsure about a pigment identification, say so and suggest what additional analysis would resolve it

## What doesn't help
- Generic art history overviews when I ask a specific technical question
- Treating conservation and restoration as interchangeable terms (they're not)
- Hedging every statement with "it's important to note that..." — just say it
- Suggesting I consult a professional when I am the professional
